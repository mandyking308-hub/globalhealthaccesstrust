PURPOSE AND RELATIONSHIP WITH THE CONSTITUTION

Global Health Access Trust builds on charitable and community work carried out since 2019. The organisation was formally established with effect from 1 December 2024. Its Trustees subsequently signed the Constitution in June 2025, confirming the governance and operating arrangements effective from that date.

This Updated Trust Deed records the supporting legal and property-holding framework of the Trust as at 24 July 2026 and is to be read alongside the signed Constitution.

Document hierarchy

The signed Constitution is the principal public reference for governance, Board arrangements, meetings, membership, finance, amendment and dissolution.

This Updated Trust Deed supports the legal and charitable-property framework and must be interpreted consistently with the Constitution.

Trustee resolutions, policies and financial controls operate beneath both documents and may not contradict them.

Applicable law prevails where any document is inconsistent with a mandatory legal requirement.

Updated Trust Deed

Global Health Access Trust

Updated on 24 July 2026 to record the Trust's supporting legal and governance framework, its formal establishment with effect from 1 December 2024, and its relationship with the signed Constitution adopted in June 2025.

1. Name and identity

1.1 The name of the organisation is Global Health Access Trust, also referred to as GHAT or the Trust.

1.2 The Trust was formally established in England and Wales with effect from 1 December 2024 and builds on charitable and community work carried out since 2019.

1.3 The Trust operates exclusively for charitable purposes and for the public benefit.

1.4 The correspondence address is Global Health Access Trust, 2 Harley Street, London, England, United Kingdom, or such other correspondence address as the Trustees may record.

2. Trustees and administration

2.1 The Trust is administered by its Board of Trustees. The Trustees act collectively and are responsible for safeguarding the charitable purposes, assets, reputation and lawful operation of the Trust.

2.2 The Trustees recorded in the signed Constitution are:

  • Mandy King - Chair of Trustees / Founder
  • Dr Jagdev Thukral - Executive Lead / Clinical Governance; and
  • John O'Sullivan BA FCA - Finance and Strategic Investment

    • 2.3 A person later appointed or removed as a Trustee must be dealt with in accordance with the signed Constitution, applicable law and a properly recorded Board decision.

    2.4 No Trustee owns any part of the Trust Fund. All Trust property is held and administered solely for the charitable purposes set out in this document and the signed Constitution.

    3. Charitable objects

    3.1 The principal object is to improve access to quality healthcare and mental health support across underserved populations globally, particularly through community outreach, education, responsible digital health innovation and capacity building.

    3.2 The Trust may carry out work in any part of the world where the Trustees are satisfied that the proposed activity is lawful, advances the object and provides a clear public benefit.

    3.3 Nothing in this document authorises the application of Trust property for a purpose that is not exclusively charitable under the law of England and Wales.

    4. Trust Fund and application of assets

    4.1 The Trust Fund comprises all money, grants, donations, investments, equipment, intellectual property, land and other assets received, acquired or held for the purposes of Global Health Access Trust, together with all income and proceeds arising from them.

    4.2 The Trustees must apply the income and, where appropriate, the capital of the Trust Fund solely in furtherance of the charitable objects.

    4.3 Restricted funds must be used only for the lawful restriction accepted by the Trustees and must be recorded separately from unrestricted funds.

    4.4 A pledge, proposed gift or promised resource is not treated as an asset of the Trust until it has been formally accepted, received and recorded.

    4.5 No part of the Trust Fund may be distributed to a Trustee, member or private person except as a lawful reimbursement, authorised payment or incidental benefit permitted by charity law.

    5. Powers

    5.1 In furtherance of the charitable objects, and not for any other purpose, the Trustees may:

  • 5.1.1 raise funds, invite and accept contributions, grants, gifts, legacies and resources;
  • 5.1.2 operate services and projects directly or in partnership with charities, public bodies, community organisations, educational institutions, healthcare providers and other suitable organisations;
  • 5.1.3 employ staff, appoint consultants, engage volunteers and obtain professional advice;
  • 5.1.4 acquire, lease, maintain, improve, manage and dispose of property and other assets where lawful and appropriate;
  • 5.1.5 open and operate bank, payment and investment accounts in the name of Global Health Access Trust;
  • 5.1.6 invest funds prudently and in accordance with applicable charity and trust law;
  • 5.1.7 make grants, provide resources and support projects that advance the charitable objects;
  • 5.1.8 create committees, advisory groups and delivery arrangements under written authority from the Board;
  • 5.1.9 develop and use technology, research, data and responsible artificial intelligence subject to appropriate human oversight, safeguarding and legal controls;
  • 5.1.10 co-operate, merge or enter joint arrangements with another charitable body where lawful and in the Trust's best interests; and
  • 5.1.11 do any other lawful thing that is necessary or desirable to advance the charitable objects.

    • 5.2 The Trustees must exercise every power in good faith, for the Trust's charitable purposes, with appropriate care and after managing conflicts of interest.

    6. Board meetings and decisions

    6.1 The Board must meet at least twice in each calendar year and may meet more frequently when the Trust's work or risks require it.

    6.2 Meetings may take place in person or by secure electronic means that permit all participating Trustees to communicate with one another.

    6.3 The quorum is no fewer than three Trustees, in accordance with the signed Constitution.

    6.4 Decisions are made by a majority of the Trustees entitled to vote. Each Trustee has one vote.

    6.5 A written resolution agreed by all Trustees is valid if it is retained with the Trust's governance records.

    6.6 Material decisions, including project approvals, significant expenditure, banking mandates, restricted funding and appointments, must be recorded in minutes or written resolutions.

    7. Delegation and operational authority

    7.1 The Board may delegate defined operational functions to designated signatory Trustees, employees, committees, professional advisers or delivery partners under written limits and reporting arrangements.

    7.2 Delegation does not remove the Board's collective responsibility and may be reviewed or withdrawn at any time.

    7.3 The Board may not delegate any decision that applicable law or the signed Constitution requires the Trustees to make collectively.

    8. Financial governance

    8.1 The Trust's financial year runs from 1 April to 31 March.

    8.2 A bank account must be maintained in the name Global Health Access Trust and operated under a recorded Board mandate.

    8.3 All income and expenditure must be recorded accurately. Restricted and unrestricted funds, project allocations and material commitments must be identifiable in the Trust's records.

    8.4 The Trustees must prepare annual financial statements and arrange the annual audit recorded in the signed Constitution, together with any additional examination, assurance or reporting required by law or the Trust's circumstances.

    8.5 The Board must maintain proportionate controls over authorisation, segregation of duties, supporting evidence, reconciliation, fraud prevention, reserves and financial sustainability.

    8.6 The Trustees may invest Trust funds only after considering suitability, diversification, liquidity, risk, the charitable timetable and appropriate professional advice.

    9. Conflicts, benefits and expenses

    9.1 Every Trustee must declare any actual or potential conflict of interest or loyalty as soon as it arises.

    9.2 A conflicted Trustee must not participate in the relevant decision or vote and must not be counted in the quorum for that decision unless the law expressly permits otherwise.

    9.3 The Trustees must maintain a register of interests and record how each material conflict was managed.

    9.4 A Trustee may be reimbursed for reasonable expenses properly incurred in carrying out Trustee duties.

    9.5 A Trustee or connected person may receive payment or another benefit from the Trust only where it is lawful, reasonable, properly authorised, transparently recorded and in the Trust's best interests.

    10. Safeguarding, regulated activity and responsible practice

    10.1 The Trustees must maintain policies and controls proportionate to the Trust's activities, including safeguarding, complaints, whistleblowing, data protection, cyber security, financial crime, sanctions, fraud, health and safety, equality, conflicts and risk management.

    10.2 Where a project includes clinical or otherwise regulated services, those services must remain under the responsibility of appropriately qualified and authorised professionals or organisations.

    10.3 Technology and artificial intelligence must support, not replace, accountable human judgment and must be used with appropriate legal, ethical, clinical and safeguarding oversight.

    11. Records and transparency

    11.1 The Trustees must retain the signed Constitution, this Updated Trust Deed, Board minutes, resolutions, trustee records, policies, contracts, financial records and material project evidence for the periods required by law and good governance.

    11.2 The Trust may publish governance, financial and project information where appropriate, while protecting personal data, safeguarding, confidentiality, legal privilege, regulated professional responsibilities and personal safety.

    11.3 A public copy of a governing or legal record may redact signatures, private residential addresses and other personal information where disclosure is unnecessary or unsafe.

    12. Amendment

    12.1 The signed Constitution may be amended by a two-thirds majority vote of the Board, as provided in the Constitution, subject to applicable law and any required regulatory consent.

    12.2 This Updated Trust Deed may be revised by a two-thirds majority of the Board to keep the supporting legal record accurate, provided that no revision may:

  • 12.2.1 make the Trust cease to be exclusively charitable;
  • 12.2.2 permit the Trust Fund to be applied for non-charitable or unauthorised private benefit;
  • 12.2.3 contradict the signed Constitution without a coordinated and lawful amendment to both records; or
  • 12.2.4 remove any protection that applicable law requires.

    • 12.3 Every amendment must be dated, recorded in the Board minutes and retained with the previous version so that the document history remains clear.

    13. Dissolution

    13.1 The Trust may be dissolved only by a properly recorded decision of the Trustees made in accordance with the signed Constitution and applicable law.

    13.2 After paying or providing for all proper liabilities, the remaining Trust Fund must be transferred to one or more charities with similar charitable objects. It must not be distributed to the Trustees or members.

    13.3 The Trustees must complete all required notifications, accounts, reports, transfers and record retention arising from the dissolution.

    14. Relationship with the signed Constitution

    14.1 The signed Constitution adopted in June 2025 sits at the centre of the Trust's governance and operations.

    14.2 This Updated Trust Deed sits alongside the Constitution as part of the wider legal and governance framework, particularly in relation to the Trust Fund, charitable-property stewardship, institutional continuity and the formal record of establishment.

    14.3 The two documents must be interpreted so that they operate together. Policies and Board decisions must be consistent with both.

    14.4 For day-to-day governance and operating procedures, the signed Constitution is the principal public reference. For the legal stewardship of Trust property and the supporting trust framework, this Updated Trust Deed is the principal public reference.

    14.5 Applicable law prevails over either document where the law imposes a mandatory requirement.

    15. Governing law

    15.1 This Updated Trust Deed and the administration of the Trust are governed by the law of England and Wales.

    15.2 The courts of England and Wales have jurisdiction, subject to the powers and jurisdiction of the Charity Commission and any other competent regulator.

    16. Review and document control

    16.1 This Updated Trust Deed is dated 24 July 2026 and records the supporting legal and governance framework current at that date.

    16.2 This updated record does not alter the Trust's formal establishment date of 1 December 2024 and does not replace the signed Constitution adopted in June 2025.

    16.3 The document should be reviewed whenever the Constitution, legal status, regulatory position, charitable objects or governance arrangements materially change.
